# Newsletter Optimization Program

## Sender
- From: Tertiary Infotech Academy Pte Ltd
- Email: newsletter@tertiaryinfotech.com
- Reply-to: enquiry@tertiaryinfotech.com

## Audience
- Singapore-based professionals and learners
- Interested in tech courses: AI, automation, data, programming
- Past attendees or people who inquired about courses
- Group: Singapore

## Goals
- Primary: Increase open rates above 40%
- Secondary: Increase click-to-open rates above 15%
- Maintain low unsubscribe rates (< 0.5%)
- Drive course registrations

## Brand Voice
- Professional but approachable
- Educational and informative
- Value-focused — highlight what learners will gain
- Singapore context (mention SkillsFuture, WSQ where relevant)

## Content Constraints
- Subject lines must start with "<<ADV>> " (Singapore legal requirement)
- Newsletter promotes training courses (n8n, AI, automation, data, etc.)
- Include course highlights, learning outcomes, and registration CTA
- Keep content scannable with headers and bullet points
- Always include a "REGISTER" CTA button linking to the course page

## Optimization Priorities
1. **Subject line experimentation** (after the required <<ADV>> prefix)
   - Test benefit-driven vs curiosity-driven approaches
   - Test including course duration, skill level, or outcomes
2. **Preview text optimization** (complements subject line)
3. **Content structure** (paragraph length, headings, bullet points)
4. **Call-to-action placement and wording** (drives registrations)
5. **Send time experimentation** (day of week, time of day)

## Current Strategy
- Course promotion newsletters
- Single course focus per email
- Bold header + hero image + CTA button layout
- Social links + company info in footer

## Off Limits
- No misleading subject lines or false urgency
- No excessive emoji in subject lines (max 1)
- No all-caps subject lines (except the existing CTA style)
- Must maintain "<<ADV>>" prefix in subject lines
- Never remove unsubscribe link or company contact info
- Never sacrifice brand trust for short-term metric gains
